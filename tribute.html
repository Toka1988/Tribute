Nelson Mandela

The man who Freed a billion lives

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in the village of Mvezo in Umtata
  • 1933 - Mandela began his secondary education.
  • 1939 - With Jongintaba's backing, Mandela began working on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare.
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1943 - Mandela met Anton Lembede, an ANC member affilated with the "Africanist" branch of African nationalism.
  • 1947 - Mandela was elected to the executive committee of the ANC's Transvaal Province branch.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1962 - Police captured Mandela along with fellow activist Cecil Williams near Howick.
  • 1990 - Mandela's unconditional release was announced.For the first time in 20 years, photographs of Mandela were allowed to be published in South Africa.
  • 1994 - The ANC began campaigning, opening 100 election offices and orchestrating People's Forums across the country at which Mandela could appear, as a popular figure with great status among black South Africans.
  • 1994 - The newly elected National Assembly's first act was to formally elect Mandela as South Africa's first black chief executive. His inauguration took place in Pretoria on 10 May 1994, televised to a billion viewers globally. The event was attended by four thousand guests, including world leaders from a wide range of geographic and ideological backgrounds.
  • 1997 - receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1998 - helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1999 - Retires from Presidency.
  • 2005 - states "we will have to double the world food supply by 2050." Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out of arable land. Says that GM crops are not inherently dangerous because "we've been genetically modifying plants and animals for a long time. Long before we called it science, people were selecting the best breeds."
  • 2013 - dies at the age of 95.

"I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons will live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to see realised. But if it needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die."

-- Mandela's Rivonia speech
